=== POST 2: last-carats-at-diavik ===
A diamond giant is selling its leftovers.

Rio Tinto's Diavik mine in Canada's Northwest Territories reached the end of its life in March. But the second quarter still produced 33,000 carats — recovered from ore already sitting on surface. Output: down 97%. Final sales run through December. Then one of the world's great mining houses exits diamonds entirely.

Argyle in 2020. Fort à la Corne in 2023. Diavik in 2026. And here's the wrinkle: Rio Tinto is keeping its diamond exploration team in Angola. Leaving the mine — not the idea. Full story, link in bio.

=== POST 3: more-dollars-fewer-boxes ===
You're paying 19% more for jewelry — and buying less of it.

America's jewelry counters grew revenue 8.6% in the first half, 13% in June alone. Sounds like a boom. But the average purchase price rose 19% — and the number of pieces sold keeps falling, especially under $1,500. Growth is coming from fewer, wealthier buyers, not more of them.

Bracelets led at +14%. Pearls quietly outperformed. Natural diamonds grew on price; lab-grown grew on units. Same showcase, two different businesses.
