=== POST 2: luk-fook-split ===

Chow Sang Sang told the same story at +16%. Both chains are still CLOSING stores — 148 and 49 net. Fewer, better doors, selling metal first.

=== POST 3: venetia-union ===
"Workers cannot be treated as disposable tools."

That's the National Union of Mineworkers answering De Beers' plan to switch off Venetia — South Africa's largest diamond mine — for two years. 1,134 mine employees and 80 sales staff are now in retrenchment consultation. The union says the company saw this downturn coming and presented a done deal as a sudden necessity.

Their counterproposal: start the cuts in the executive suite. And all of it lands while De Beers itself is being sold to Gareth Penny's consortium.

=== POST 4: sixty-one-percent ===
61% of couples who married in 2025 chose a lab-grown center stone. Not 6%. Sixty-one.

That's The Knot's 2026 Real Weddings study — and the economics explain it fast: lab-grown wholesale fell another 14-15% in Q1, and a premium one-carat lab stone now retails for $800-$1,200. A comparable natural? Thousands more.

Analyst Sherry Smith says the market isn't either/or anymore — it's bifurcated. Lab-grown owns value. Natural owns rarity. Both can win.
