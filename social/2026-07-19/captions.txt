=== POST 1: gold-war-week ===
Gold just logged its worst week in six — while missiles were flying.

Spot closed Friday at $3,985.80, down about 3.4% on the week, even as strikes on Iran entered day six. The reason: war pushed oil to one-month highs, oil means inflation, and inflation now means rate-hike bets — 73% odds of a December move by Friday's close. Higher rates are the one force gold has never outrun.

The war premium didn't disappear. It inverted. Full breakdown on the trade paper — link in bio.

=== POST 2: rolex-reject-pile ===
Nearly two in five Rolexes offered to one major marketplace never make it past inspection.

Bezel says 38% of submitted Rolexes fail authentication — counterfeits, Frankenstein builds, misdescribed or outright stolen watches. Omega runs at 13%, Tudor 6%, Cartier 5%. Measured in money, rejected Rolexes are 43% of all value refused.

The uncomfortable math: if that's the reject rate WITH professional inspection, what are the odds on a private deal with none? Full story — link in bio.

=== POST 3: resonance-serial-022 ===
He chose the serial number to match his birth date. Then he kept the watch for 27 years.

F.P. Journe Chronomètre à Résonance no. 022/99R — 38mm platinum, pink-gold dial, one of roughly ten pre-production examples — is almost certainly the first Résonance ever commercially delivered. The first twenty went to subscription supporters; 021 stayed with Journe himself. This November, Phillips offers 022 in Geneva at CHF 500,000–1,000,000.

The estimate may be a formality: Phillips has sold three historic Résonances this year for $23M+ combined, one at $13.9M. Full story — link in bio.

=== POST 4: ai-venix-debate ===
Guess just shipped a watch partly designed by a machine — and it looks like money.

The AI Venix: $250, 200 pieces, 42mm tonneau case in plastic with an AI-generated print flowing across case, strap and box. WatchPro's verdict: it "might be mistaken for a Richard Mille from across the room." The design language of a six-figure watch, drawn by an algorithm, priced like a dinner.

Here's the real question: luxury has always claimed design is the one thing you can't automate. Is 'AI-designed' a feature you'd pay for — or the exact moment you'd walk away? Comments open. Full story — link in bio.

=== POST 5: hamptons-seventy-dollars ===
The jewelry trade is skipping the convention center and pitching a tent where the money summers.

The second annual Hamptons Jewelry Show runs July 23–26 at the Southampton Fairgrounds — 36,000 square feet, a $200 VIP preview Thursday evening, $70 day passes after. New this year: a Piazza Italia pavilion for Italian goldsmithing and a Studio Artists Galleria, with names from Parisian watchmaker Charles Oudin to German Kabirski joining the returning dealers.

It's built like an art fair, not a trade show — and if it holds for a second summer, expect copies in Aspen and Palm Beach. Full preview — link in bio.
