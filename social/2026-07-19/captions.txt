=== POST 1: war-premium-inverts ===
Gold's oldest rule just broke. 🪖📉

For two generations, war meant one thing on the metals desk: buy gold. This week delivered a sixth straight day of airstrikes on Iranian targets — and gold fell 3.4%, its worst week in six, closing at $3,985.80.

The mechanism: strikes near the Strait of Hormuz pushed oil to one-month highs. High oil reads as INFLATION now, not fear — and December rate-hike odds hit 73%. A central bank that might tighten raises real yields, and real yields are the gravity no gold rally escapes.

For the trade there's quiet relief: benches costing jewelry against $3,986 instead of June's $4,100 — a $100/oz discount arriving exactly as fall orders go in.

=== POST 2: thirty-eight-percent ===
Nearly 2 in 5 Rolexes offered to a professional marketplace can't survive inspection. 🚨⌚

Bezel says 38% of Rolex watches submitted to its platform FAIL authentication — counterfeits, Frankenstein builds of mismatched parts, inaccurate listings or stolen goods. Compare: Omega 13%, Tudor 6%, Cartier 5%, Patek 2-3%.

Why Rolex? The skew tracks demand. Rolex is 28% of what sells and 34% of what buyers want — the crown draws forgers the way it draws everyone else. By VALUE, rejected Rolexes are 43% of everything refused; Patek adds another 22%.

The takeaway: if 38% fail with professional authenticators, imagine the odds on a private deal with no inspection at all.

=== POST 3: serial-022 ===
He chose the serial number himself — 022, his birth date. Then he kept the watch for 27 years. 🕰️

This November, F.P. Journe Chronomètre à Résonance no. 022/99R comes to Phillips' Geneva Watch Auction: XXIV, estimated at CHF 500,000–1,000,000. Phillips calls it almost certainly the FIRST Résonance ever commercially delivered — serials 001–020 went to subscribers, 021 stayed with Journe himself.

The market it enters: Phillips has sold three historic Résonance examples in 2026 for a combined $23M+, topped by the Souscription No. 007 at $13.9M in May. Journe now trades in territory once reserved for vintage Patek.

Single owner, pre-production, self-selected serial. This is the kind of lot that detonates estimates.

=== POST 4: ap-105-paradox ===
Audemars Piguet sold £105 MILLION of watches in Britain last year. Profit? £7.8 million — and falling. 🧮

UK sales jumped 29% in 2025, close to double the 2022 number. Operating profit went the OTHER way: down 3%. The gap is a property strategy: the Manchester AP House booked £7.4M in sales in its first year and still LOST £738,000. Fixtures on the UK balance sheet went from £2.8M to £17.6M in a single year.

This is the sharp end of luxury's big shift: brands pulling distribution inward — trading wholesale margin paid to retailers for the full retail margin AND the full retail cost base. Carpeted lounges, appointment books, in-house hospitality.

The open question: does £17.6M of fixtures earn its keep across a full cycle?
