=== POST 1: penny-de-beers ===
De Beers has a buyer — and he used to run the place.

Anglo American has named the Global Diamond Consortium, led by former De Beers chief executive Gareth Penny, as preferred bidder for its 85% stake. The announcement came in Botswana's parliament, not a London boardroom — and that detail is the whole story. Botswana owns 15%, holds the right to match, and the consortium wants Angola and Namibia at the table.

If the funding closes, the world's most famous diamond company could end up owned by the countries that mine its diamonds. Close expected Q4 2026.

=== POST 2: gold-4000-test ===
$4,000 was tested twice this week. It held twice.

Gold's August futures opened Friday at $3,980 — under the line — on the sixth straight day of strikes on Iranian targets. By the New York close: $4,031, up 1.2%. The strange part? War isn't buying gold a premium anymore. Rate-hike odds are doing the pricing now, and the Fed meets in ten days.

Gold is still 28% below January's $5,595 peak — and still up 20% on the year. Both things are true. The market is deciding which to price first.

=== POST 3: five-pound-ring ===
She paid £5 for it, thinking it was costume jewelry. It's a 3.02-carat natural diamond.

At a WeBuyVintage valuation event in Oxfordshire, the loupe delivered the news: 18-karat gold, natural diamond, roughly £15,000. A three-thousand-fold return on a car boot sale gamble.

Why does this keep happening? Old cuts read as dull to eyes trained on modern brilliants. Estates empty into markets faster than knowledge follows — and real goods circulate unrecognized until someone finally looks properly.

=== POST 5: trophy-watch-heat ===
90% of lots sold. 45% beat their high estimates. The trophy watch market has cleared the room.

Sotheby's watch sales are running 64% ahead of last year, per global head Geoff Hess. The receipts: a Paul Newman Daytona at $1.14M, an F.P. Journe with Tom Brady provenance at $1.02M — and Simon Brette, a brand five years old, at $512,000.

The heat has moved from hype references to independents, neo-vintage and vintage Cartier. This is collector money, not flipper money — slower, stickier, and much more selective.
