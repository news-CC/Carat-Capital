=== POST 1: gold-floor ===
Gold's $4,000 floor has been hit three times — and held three times. The fourth test comes with a Fed meeting attached. 📉

Friday looked like the break: spot traded down to $3,959.30, five dollars off the triple bottom every technician is watching. Then the late save — a $42 rally into the close at $4,017.30, and a Monday open at $4,018.

The tug-of-war: oil at $90 feeds inflation, inflation feeds rate-hike odds (December now ~82%), and higher rates tax gold. Meanwhile silver is quietly doing the work — up 1.67% Monday morning while gold argues with the Fed.

July 28–29. Mark it.

=== POST 2: botswana-cards ===
The De Beers sale has a preferred bidder. Botswana just reminded everyone it has something better: a veto. ♟️🇧🇼

Anglo American selected the Global Diamond Consortium — led by ex-De Beers CEO Gareth Penny, with a Qatari fund and businessman Nir Livnat alongside — as preferred bidder for its 85%. Botswana's minister answered in parliament: the state's 15% carries "complete freedom" — JOIN the consortium, exercise pre-emption ALONE, or bid with a partner of its own choosing.

The stakes are national, not corporate: diamonds are ~80% of Botswana's exports and a third of state revenue, and President Boko's government openly wants a larger — even controlling — stake.

Six bidders became two. Target close: Q4 — subject, prominently, to Gaborone's approval.

=== POST 3: ruby-tape ===
The colored-stone trade has always envied one thing about diamonds: a printed price. Rubies just got one. 💎🔴

Gemfields' first 'Trade Select' auction — a deliberately broader mix of Mozambique ruby qualities than its premium sales — cleared 348,409 of 374,008 carats offered (93.2% by weight) for $23.1 MILLION, at an average of $66.30 a carat. 82 of 89 lots sold. Sapphire categories debuted alongside for the first time.

Why it matters: the mid-grade color business has historically bought through opaque dealer chains. An open tender with published results gives manufacturers and calibrated-goods cutters something they've never had — price discovery.

Meanwhile the top tier stays careful: May's emerald auction made $26.8M at $146.08/carat, 99% sold — but with buyers cautious and the rupee 10% weaker against the trade's biggest cutting hub.
