=== POST 1: canada-fifty-tariff ===
The tariff wall just reached the diamond counter.

A 50% tariff on Canadian goods takes effect August 19 — and it names the jewelry trade directly: loose polished diamonds, silver jewelry, gold chains, precious-metal and even base-metal jewelry. Six HTS codes in all.

The twist: rough diamonds are exempt. Canadian stones cut in India enter as Indian goods. What pays the 50% is the stone actually polished in Canada — the traceable, mine-to-market goods the ethical-sourcing movement was built on.

=== POST 2: gold-peak-rejected ===
Gold's rally met its counterparty — a barrel of crude.

Spot backed off Wednesday's two-week peak of $4,165.87 to $4,103 Thursday morning. The culprit wasn't the dollar (it eased). It was oil at a six-week high, dragging inflation expectations and rate-hike odds up with it: CME futures now put 77% on a September hike.

The strange loop: the same Middle East risk that buys gold its haven bid is selling it back through the oil market. The Fed meets July 28–29.

=== POST 3: swatch-split-screen ===
The group behind Omega, Tissot and Harry Winston just posted the strangest half in Swiss watchmaking.

Top line: a real recovery. Revenue up 9% at constant currency, the US up 27%, Japan up 20%, Spain up 28% — and China, the market that defined three bad years, back to +9%.

Bottom line: net profit of CHF 16 million on CHF 3.12 billion of sales. That's a margin of half a percent. The Swiss franc converts foreign revenue into fewer francs; the factories stay resolutely Swiss.

=== POST 4: mrk-lemon-debate ===
The most talked-about lemon in American jewelry costs $21,650 — and it opens.

Monica Rich Kosann's Enchanted Garden series: a cherry ($10,985) hiding a cabochon garnet, a pear ($14,500) concealing five garnets, and a lemon ($21,650, special order only) that splits on its hinge to reveal white-diamond seeds.

At the top of the market, jewelry is drifting from adornment toward miniature sculpture — houses competing on hinges and hidden compartments as much as on carats.

So: cheeky masterpiece or $21,650 too far? Comments are open.

=== POST 5: rolex-tysons-flex ===
The Washington suburbs now have a Rolex address to match their tax bracket.

Liljenquist & Beckstead — family jewelers since 1978 — opened a 2,800-square-foot Rolex boutique on Tysons Galleria's main corridor: sawtooth bronze-brass storefront, American walnut walls, travertine display windows.

The centerpiece: a Rolex-trained watchmaking workshop behind glass, with a dry bar positioned so you can watch a movement being serviced while you wait. Service, promoted to the shop window.
