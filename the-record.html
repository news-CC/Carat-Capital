The RecordEight weeks of the trade, kept properly

Eight weeks of the trade, kept the way a ledger should be kept — dated, sourced, and in one place. New entries are added at the top each week; the quarter's running numbers live in the Almanac below.

This week — July 20

the week's ledger
Gold & Metals

The $4,000 floor survives its third test

Spot gold closed Friday 17 July at $4,017.30, up $42.10 on the day, after touching $3,959.30 — within five dollars of the triple bottom at $3,964. Monday opened at $4,018.20 with December hike odds near 82%, Brent at $90.40 and the Fed's July 28–29 meeting ahead. Silver led the complex at $56.76, +1.67%.

Source — Kitco / FX Leaders
Diamonds

India's June exports jump 26.5% — the factory restarts

Gross gems and jewellery exports reached $2,212.6M in June per GJEPC data: gold jewellery +54.5% to $1,087.7M, cut-and-polished diamonds +8.7% to $846.7M. The quarter stayed flat (+0.04% at $6,612.2M) with polished value −4.1% on volume +8.9% — $681.70 a carat; rough imports −32.4% by value.

Source — GJEPC / IANS
Diamonds

Botswana keeps all three doors open on the De Beers sale

Minister Moeti Mohwasa told parliament on 17 July that the state's 15% carries 'complete freedom': partner with Anglo's preferred bidder — the Gareth Penny-led Global Diamond Consortium, with a Qatari fund and Nir Livnat — pre-empt alone, or bid with a third party. Two bidders remain from six; close eyed by Q4.

Source — Reuters / Mining Weekly
Gemstones

Gemfields prices the ruby middle market at $66.30

The miner's first 'Trade Select' auction — a broader mix of Mozambique ruby qualities plus debut sapphire categories — sold 348,409 of 374,008 carats (93.2%) for $23.1M, 82 of 89 lots. May's higher-quality emerald sale made $26.8M at $146.08/ct with buyers cautious and the rupee ~10% weaker since September.

Source — Gemfields / Sharecast
Diamonds

Alrosa certifies 29.8M carats carbon-neutral — for the second year

TÜV Austria verified the Russian miner's operations and full 2025 production as carbon-neutral: footprint −1.18 kg CO2e per carat (60% better), facilities absorbing 126,000 tonnes more CO2 than emitted, 76% renewable electricity. G7 import restrictions still bar the goods from Western markets.

Source — Rapaport

Week of July 13

the week's ledger
Gold & Metals

Gold logs its worst week in six — during a war

Spot closed 17 July at $3,985.80, down ~3.4% on the week as a sixth day of strikes on Iran drove oil to one-month highs and CME December hike odds to 73%; Dallas Fed's Logan called for a hike. Silver $55.20; platinum $1,599; palladium $1,244.

Source — USAGOLD / CNBC / Yahoo Finance
Auctions

First-ever delivered F.P. Journe Résonance consigned to Phillips Geneva XXIV

No. 022/99R — 38mm platinum, one owner since 1999, serial chosen to match his birth date — estimated CHF 500,000–1M for 7–8 Nov. Phillips' three historic Résonance sales in 2026 total $23M+, incl. Souscription No. 007 at $13.9M.

Source — WatchPro
Watches

Audemars Piguet UK: sales +29% to £105M, profit down 3%

2025 accounts show £7.8M operating profit on £105M sales; Manchester AP House booked £7.4M sales and a £738K loss in year one; UK property and fittings rose from £2.8M to £17.6M. Clifton Street AP House opened Feb 2026.

Source — WatchPro
Retail & Tech

Bezel rejects 38% of submitted Rolexes at authentication

Counterfeits, Frankenwatches, misdescriptions and stolen goods; Omega 13%, Tudor 6%, Cartier 5%, most others 2–3%. Rejected Rolexes are 43% of refused value; Patek 22%. Rolex is 28% of Bezel sales and 34% of wish lists.

Source — WatchPro USA
Gemstones

Brandee Dallow starts as ICA chief executive

Effective 6 July; the 1984-founded International Colored Gemstone Association counts ~700 members in 47 countries. Dallow arrives via De Beers, Rio Tinto Diamonds, RJC, Grandview Klein and the Women's Jewelry Association.

Source — The Centurion / IDEX
Diamonds

Anglo names Penny's Global Diamond Consortium preferred bidder for De Beers

Announced to Botswana's parliament 17 July by minister Moeti Mohwasa; three shortlisted bidders; consortium plans Angola and Namibia as partners; Botswana (15%) weighs matching rights; close eyed Q4 2026. De Beers carried at $4.9B after $3.5B impairments.

Source — Rapaport / Bloomberg / Miningmx
Retail & Tech

India–UK trade pact enters force; jewelry goes duty-free both ways

CETA effective 15 July; UK scraps duties on 96.8% of tariff lines (97.7% of trade value); first zero-duty gold, silver and diamond jewelry consignments shipped 16 July. India→UK goods exports $13.44B in FY25–26; 900+ jewelry firms expected to benefit.

Source — Al Jazeera / AajTak / Outlook Business
Watches

Rolex CPO premium compresses to 22.6% median as program scales

Morgan Stanley × WatchCharts: launch-era ~40% premium now 22.6% median; Q2 program sales $186M; 157 of 712 retailers enrolled; top-three inventory share down from 63% to 48%; median participating retailer's H1 CPO revenue $899k, from $578k.

Source — WatchPro / Morgan Stanley–WatchCharts
Auctions

Sotheby's watch sales run 64% ahead at the half; 90% sell-through

Latest sale: 1,850 bidders from 60 countries; 45% of lots beat high estimates (double y/y); sales at 200%+ of combined low estimates. Recent prints: Paul Newman 6263 $1.14M, Journe Vagabondage II 'Brady' $1.02M, Brette Artisans Rose $512k.

Source — WatchPro (Geoff Hess interview)
Diamonds

Ekati goes to receivership; Canada's first diamond mine will close

B.C. court order 14 July at the NWT government's request; PwC receiver; production stops mid-August after an eight-week sale found no buyer. ~340 workers remain (2024: 700 + 542 contractors); C$326M reclamation security shielded; last sale C$27.4M, C$2M short.

Source — Cabin Radio / Rapaport / CBC
Diamonds

Angola's Endiama bids for all of Anglo's 85% of De Beers

Confirmed by CEO Ganga Júnior; De Beers carried at $4.9B after $3.5B of impairments, 17 months into Anglo's exit. Botswana (15%) holds the right to match; President Boko calls it a matter of economic sovereignty; ex-De Beers executive groups also bidding.

Source — Bloomberg / Mining Technology
Diamonds

Lab-grown Q2 wholesale: −13% overall, +1% at one carat

Edahn Golan's Q2 price list: 2ct rounds −20%, squares −21% (some VVS ranges −25%+), fancies −17%, 1.50–1.99ct −11%; one-carat rounds the only riser at +1%. Rough LGD prices rising — pass-through to polished is the next-quarter question.

Source — INSTORE / Edahn Golan

Source — Yahoo Finance / Trading Economics

June US retail: +0.2% to $768.6B, below forecast

Census advance estimate; tax-refund tailwinds faded, online led category gains. Jewelry's H2 setup: a deliberate consumer, gold's 21.8% YTD climb as the value script.

Source — Census / Rapaport / AP

Source — WatchPro / Richemont
